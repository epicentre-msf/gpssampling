# What is GPS Sampling

**GPS Sampling** is a web application designed for providing users with
procedures for estimating population size using geographical
information. The application is based on [R](https://www.r-project.org/)
freeware and the [Shiny](https://shiny.rstudio.com/) package.

GPS Sampling is a fork of
[GeoSampler](https://apps.msf.net/geosampler/site/index.html),
originally developed by [Epicentre](https://epicentre.msf.org/) to
support the activities of [Médecins Sans
Frontières](https://www.msf.org). This fork is maintained independently
for internal use and released under the [WTFPL](http://www.wtfpl.net/)
license.

Please use the issue tracker on GitHub to suggest enhancements or report
problems:
[https://github.com/epicentre-msf/gpssampling/issues](https://github.com/epicentre-msf/gpssampling/issues).

Three sampling methods are implemented:

- the [Quadrats](https://yves-amevoin.github.io/gpssampling/articles/)
- the [T-square](https://yves-amevoin.github.io/gpssampling/articles/)
- the [Roof-Simple-Random
  sampling](https://yves-amevoin.github.io/gpssampling/articles/)

The three methods have different limitations and potential weaknesses
depending of the context they are applied and the type and quality of
information available.
